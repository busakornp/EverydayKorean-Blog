Korean Phrases

By Busakorn
Sep 10, 2020

Unit 3: Korean Phrases

Useful Phrases for shopping

사과 있어요? – sa-kwa-ee-sor-yo – Do you have apples?

네, 있어요 – nae-ee-sor-yo – Yes, we have

얼마예요? – earl-ma-ye-yo – How much are they?

2,000 원 이에요 – ee-cheon-won-i-ae-yo – They’re 2,000 won each

사과 두개 주세요 – sa-kwa-du-gae-chu-sae-yo – I’d like two apples

두 means “2”, 개 is a subject marker for objects and 주세요 actually means “give me”

Useful Phrases to use when eating

잘 먹겠습니다 – jal-mug-get-sum-ni-da – Bon appétit

잘 means “well” and 먹겠습니다 means “will eat” so the direct translation is “I will eat well”

잘 먹었습니다 – jal-mug-gut-sum-ni-da – Thank you for the meal

In this phrase, 먹었습니다 means “ate” and so the translation for this is “I ate well”

우와~ – Wow

먹자! – muk-ja – Let’s eat! (informal)

이제 먹어보시죠 – i-je meok-eo-bo-shi-jyo – Let’s eat! (formal)

맛있겠다 – mat-it-get-neun-geol – It looks yummy (informal)

맛있어보여요 – mat-it-eo-bo-yeo-yo – It looks yummy (formal)

맛있어요 – ma-si-sseo-yo – It’s yummy (formal)

배고파 – bae-go-pa – I’m hungry (informal)

배고파요 – bae-go-pa-yo – I’m hungry (formal)

배불러요 – bae-bool-law-yo – I’m full (formal)

Useful Phrases to help you get around in Korea

… 어디에 있어요? – eo-di-eh-e-seo-yo – Where’s …?

e.g. 기차역 어디에 있어요? – Where is the train station?

길을 잃었어요 – gil-ul-il-heo-sseo-yo – I’m lost

저기에 – jeo-gi-ae – Over there

근처 – gun-cheo – Neaby

여기에 – yeo-gi-ae – Here
